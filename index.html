Discover the most stunning sights of Budapest

Hungarian Parliament Building

The Hungarian Parliament Building is one of the most iconic landmarks of Budapest, and it's also the largest building in Hungary. Completed in 1904, this neo-gothic masterpiece stands on the bank of the Danube and offers stunning views of the city. Visitors can take guided tours to learn about the history and architecture of the building.

Buda Castle

Buda Castle is a historic palace complex located on the Buda side of the city, overlooking the Danube River. Originally built in the 14th century, the castle has undergone many changes and renovations over the centuries. Today, it houses several museums and galleries, including the Hungarian National Gallery and the Budapest History Museum.

Learn more about Buda Castle

Fisherman's Bastion

Fisherman's Bastion is a unique architectural and historical gem of Budapest. It is a terrace located on the Buda Castle hill, offering panoramic views of the city and the Danube. The bastion was built between 1895 and 1902 and is named after the guild of fishermen who were responsible for defending this stretch of the city walls in the Middle Ages. Visitors can explore the seven turrets, each of which represents one of the seven Magyar tribes that settled in the Carpathian Basin in the 9th century.
